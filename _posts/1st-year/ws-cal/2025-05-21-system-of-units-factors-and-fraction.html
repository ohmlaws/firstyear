Q5: What is the LCM of 12, 18, 6, 36?

  • 12
  • 18
  • 36
  • 42
Show Calculation

\[ \begin{array}{r|cccc} \color{red}2 & 12, & 18, & 6, & 36 \\ \hline \color{red}2 & 6, & 9, & 3, & 18 \\ \hline \color{red}3 & 3, & 9, & 3, & 9 \\ \hline \color{red}3 & 1, & 3, & 1, & 3 \\ \hline & 1, & 1, & 1, & 1 \\ \end{array} \quad \]
\[ \text{LCM} = 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 = 36 \]

Q7: What is the improper fraction for the given mixed fraction \(7 \dfrac{3}{7}\)?

  • 20/5
  • 5/2
  • 4/3
  • 52/7
Show Calculation

To convert a mixed fraction to an improper fraction:

\( 7 \dfrac{3}{7} = \dfrac{7 \times 7 + 3}{7} = \dfrac{49 + 3}{7} = \dfrac{52}{7} \)

Q9: Simplify: \(\frac{3}{4} + \frac{2}{5} - \frac{5}{20}\)

  • \(\frac{9}{10}\)
  • \(\frac{13}{10}\)
  • \(\frac{23}{20}\)
  • \(\frac{25}{20}\)
Show Calculation

Convert all terms to have denominator 20:

\[ \frac{3}{4} = \frac{15}{20},\quad \frac{2}{5} = \frac{8}{20},\quad \frac{5}{20} = \frac{5}{20} \]

Now compute:
\[ \frac{15 + 8 - 5}{20} = \frac{18}{20} = \frac{\cancel{18}^{^{9}}}{\cancel{20}^{^{10}}} = \frac{9}{10} \]

Q10: Divide: \(\frac{20}{31} \div \frac{15}{62}\)

  • \(2 \frac{4}{3}\)
  • \(2 \frac{1}{3}\)
  • \(2 \frac{3}{2}\)
  • \(2 \frac{2}{3}\)
Show Calculation

\( \frac{20}{31} \div \frac{15}{62} = \frac{\cancel{20}^{^{4}}}{\cancel{31}} \times \frac{\cancel{62}^{^{2}}}{\cancel{15}^{^{3}}} = \frac{8}{3} \)

Convert to mixed number:
\( \frac{8}{3} = 2 \frac{2}{3} \)

Q12: Simplify: \(\frac{17.49 \times 5.2}{6.5}\)

  • 13.69
  • 13.79
  • 13.89
  • 13.99
Show Calculation

Step 1: Multiply the numerator:
\[ 17.49 \times 5.2 = 90.948 \]
Step 2: Divide by 6.5:
\[ \frac{90.948}{6.5} = 13.992 \approx 13.99 \]
Final Answer: \(\boxed{13.99}\)
